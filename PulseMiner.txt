# PulseMiner

**PulseMiner** is an ethical AI-powered public sentiment observatory that helps organizations understand what people care about — **without relying only on traditional polls**.

Instead of depending exclusively on direct survey responses, PulseMiner combines multiple **public, consensual, and contextual signals** to estimate public mood, issue salience, and emerging concerns across regions and over time.

It is designed for use in:
- civic intelligence
- policy analysis
- media monitoring
- NGO field awareness
- social research
- development programs
- public opinion infrastructure

---

## Vision

Public opinion is often measured too slowly, too narrowly, or too expensively.

PulseMiner aims to become a **real-time public mood infrastructure** by ethically fusing signals such as:

- public social conversation
- public comments and discussions
- search-interest trends
- news attention
- first-party micro-responses
- anonymous voice submissions
- contextual open data

The goal is not to spy on people, but to build a system that can answer questions like:

- What are people most worried about this week?
- Which issues are rising fastest in a given region?
- Is public frustration increasing or easing?
- What themes dominate public conversation right now?
- What changed after a major event or policy announcement?

---

## Core Idea

PulseMiner estimates a latent public mood signal by combining multiple observed signals.

A simple conceptual form is:

\[
M_{r,t,k} = f(P_{r,t,k}, T_{r,t,k}, W_{r,t,k}, V_{r,t,k}, N_{r,t,k}, C_{r,t,k})
\]

Where:

- \(M_{r,t,k}\) = inferred public mood for region \(r\), time \(t\), and issue \(k\)
- \(P\) = public post signal
- \(T\) = search trend signal
- \(W\) = micro-widget response signal
- \(V\) = voice-note topic/sentiment signal
- \(N\) = news attention signal
- \(C\) = contextual signal

This turns fragmented public traces into an interpretable and uncertainty-aware civic intelligence layer.

## Key Principles

### 1. Ethical by Design
PulseMiner does **not** depend on invasive surveillance, private data extraction, or hidden tracking.

### 2. Public + Consensual First
We prioritize:
- public content that is already openly available
- first-party signals users intentionally provide
- aggregated contextual indicators

### 3. Privacy-Aware Aggregation
The system is designed to work on **aggregated patterns**, not on exposing or profiling individuals.

### 4. Uncertainty Matters
Public sentiment is noisy and biased. PulseMiner should always expose:
- confidence levels
- source coverage
- representativeness caveats
- missing-data warnings

### 5. Sentiment is Not the Population
Online conversation is not identical to true population opinion. The platform should clearly distinguish:
- **observed online sentiment**
- **estimated broader public mood**
- **high-confidence vs low-confidence inferences**

## Product Overview

PulseMiner consists of five major layers:

### A. Data Ingestion Layer
Collects approved signals from multiple sources.

Possible inputs:
- public social posts
- public comment streams
- search trend data
- news feeds
- embedded micro-pulse widgets
- anonymous voice submissions
- contextual open datasets

### B. Processing Layer
Normalizes, cleans, filters, and enriches raw inputs.

Tasks include:
- deduplication
- spam filtering
- language detection
- translation
- topic extraction
- sentiment scoring
- urgency detection
- region inference
- timestamp alignment

### C. Signal Fusion Layer
Combines multiple cleaned signals into one or more inferred indicators.

Examples:
- public frustration score
- optimism score
- issue salience score
- policy support score
- trust score
- anomaly index

### D. Analytics Layer
Produces dashboards, time series, heatmaps, weekly summaries, and alerts.

### E. Delivery Layer
Exposes insights via:
- web dashboard
- API
- exportable reports
- internal admin tools
- partner-facing widgets

## Ethics and Safety

This project should include a dedicated `docs/ethics.md` and treat ethics as a core system requirement, not a side note.

### Non-Negotiables
- no scraping of private communities
- no hidden surveillance
- no deanonymization
- no sale of identifiable citizen data
- no precise geolocation without clear consent
- no pretending model output equals ground truth

### Required Safeguards
- source allowlist
- consent tracking for first-party submissions
- automated PII filtering
- aggregation thresholds before display
- rate limits and abuse detection
- audit logs for ingestion pipelines
- transparent methodology notes

### Reporting Rules
Dashboards should communicate:
- what the indicator means
- what it does not mean
- which sources contributed
- level of confidence
- known biases and blind spots

## Methodological Caution

PulseMiner does not claim to directly read the minds of a population.

It estimates public mood from available signals, and those signals can be biased.

Therefore every serious release should include:
- methodology summary
- source coverage statement
- uncertainty explanation
- known limitations
- ethical use notice

---

## Why This Matters

Most systems today either:
- wait for formal surveys,
- scrape noisy online content without rigor,
- or produce dashboards without methodological honesty.

PulseMiner aims to be different:
- ethically grounded
- multilingual
- region-aware
- uncertainty-aware
- useful in real-world decision environments

This is not just sentiment analysis.

It is infrastructure for understanding public concern, public mood, and issue salience in near real time.
